WHAT EVEN IS A PWA?

It’s not a website. It’s not an app. It’s a secret third thing.

"A PWA is cached HTML of the target site that updates. You open a shortcut with an icon/name described via a manifest saved with the site."

Upon clicking this icon, the browser renders the cached HTML. It doesn't ask the server for permission. It just opens.

The Service Worker (a script living in your browser) occasionally updates that cache in the background. It keeps things fresh while you sleep.

No App Store. No 200MB downloads. No "Update Available" popups. Just the web, but it lives on your phone now.

Best of all, it works anywhere with a browser. There is no reliance on proprietary nonsense executables, as its literally just HTML and Javascript!
